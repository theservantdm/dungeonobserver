Just what is this "Fragility System"?

In short, it's an alternate to the d20 System designed around the other types of polyhedral dice.

The goal of the system was to use the natural properties of the dice to refect character progression and to have a flexible and intuative system for resolving events. Also, to incorporate character fatigue and damage as a factor into in-game outcomes, and to have a better spectrum of character abilities to draw on for event resolution.

Alright, I'm interested, how does it work?

The player character's ability scores are measured by the type of polyhedral dice (1d4 to 2d12). Dice are rolled against a success criteria of getting above a 3. Thus a d4 has a 25% chance of succes, a d12 has a 75% of success, 2d6 have a 50% chance of 1 success and an additional 25% chance of a double success, etc.

In addition to this, creatures can acquire fatigue and wound dice which count as a negative success if they roll above a 3. Fatigue dice are d6's (50% chance) and wound dice are d12's (75% chance) and can accumulate depending on character actions.

Ability checks are used to resolve uncertian outcomes, using a contested roll whenever possible, and an set number of successes when not.

An example might be throwing a punch in a barfight. The DM would call for you to roll a strength + dexterity + proficiency check contested by your opponent's dexterity + strength + armour

Ability scores are defined on two specturms: they measure your physical, psyche, and persona against force, finesse, and fortitude (yes, I deserve an A+ for the alliteration).
